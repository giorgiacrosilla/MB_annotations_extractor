Saturday, December 22, 1894, London
I was ill, but we did some errands - getting desks, among other things, and met Loeser at lunch.
He and B.B. went to the Luxembourg and I packed. I crossed on Friday night, and had a horrible crossing.
Friday, January 11, 1895, Lion d’Or, Reims
A strange Christmas tide - now happily over. I will not write of it at present, for all is well again, and I mean it to continue so. I left the children listening to the “Mysterious Island”. They kissed me goodbye under the impression that I was an octopus, I believe, or else an ourang-outang or a pirate. Evelyn came to see me off, and there met Bernhard and Obrist.
