After lunch we read the Introduction to Marshall’s Pain, Pleasure and Aesthetics, and fell into a quarrel over the definition of pleasure. The wrangle was renewed in the evening, and only after much bitterness of soul did we each succeed in modifying our statements sufficiently to admit that a definition of pleasure is still to seek, the nearest we can arrive at being: Pleasure is the feeling accompanying the attainment of the sort of life we desire, or the confirmation of our sense of capacity to attain it.
